Frequently asked questions about AI agents and how they can improve your business operations.

What is an AI agent?

An AI agent is an autonomous software program that perceives its environment, makes decisions, and takes actions to accomplish goals. Unlike chatbots that follow scripts, AI agents reason through problems, use multiple tools, and handle multi-step tasks without human intervention.

How are AI agents different from chatbots?

Chatbots respond to anticipated questions with pre-programmed answers. AI agents understand context, make autonomous decisions, connect to your business tools, retain memory across conversations, and complete complex multi-step tasks independently.

What tasks can AI agents handle?

AI agents handle customer service inquiries, lead qualification and follow-up, appointment scheduling, data processing, report generation, system monitoring, email management, research, and cross-tool coordination. Any repetitive decision-based task is a candidate.

How much does a custom AI agent cost?

Custom AI agent development ranges from $3,000 to $15,000 depending on complexity and integrations. We scope every project during a free discovery call and provide a fixed quote. Most clients see positive ROI within the first month.

How long does development take?

Most AI agents are deployed within 2 to 4 weeks. Simple single-workflow agents can go live in one week. Complex agents with multiple integrations take up to 6 weeks. We work in weekly sprints so you see progress throughout.

Can agents integrate with my tools?

Yes. Our AI agents integrate with CRMs like Salesforce and HubSpot, email platforms, calendars, databases, Slack, project management tools, payment processors, and any system with an API or webhook support.

Are AI agents secure?

We build every agent with encrypted data transmission, role-based access controls, audit logging, and compliance with data protection standards. Agents follow the principle of least privilege, accessing only the data they need for their specific tasks.

What if an agent can't handle a request?

Every agent includes intelligent human handoff protocols. When a situation exceeds the agent's capabilities or confidence threshold, it escalates to a human team member with full conversation context and a summary of actions attempted.
