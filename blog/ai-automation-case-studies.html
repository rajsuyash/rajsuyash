AI Automation Case Studies: Real Results from Real Businesses

Forget the hype. Forget the thought leadership. Here are real businesses using AI automation with measurable results. No theory, no speculation. Just numbers.

Case Study #1: Le Marquier Cuts Support Costs by 80%

The business

Le Marquier is a premium BBQ and outdoor cooking equipment retailer. They sell high-end plancha grills, smokers, and accessories to consumers across Europe. Their support team handles everything from product selection questions to troubleshooting and order tracking.

The challenge

Le Marquier's customer support team was drowning. Call volume had been climbing steadily, and by early 2025, they were fielding hundreds of calls per week. The problem was not complexity. Roughly 60% of those calls were repetitive: "Where is my order?" "Which plancha fits a 4-person household?" "How do I season my grill?" The same questions, over and over.

Support staff were burning hours on calls that did not require human judgment. Meanwhile, high-value inquiries (custom orders, B2B partnerships, technical escalations) were getting pushed to the back of the queue.

The solution

We deployed an AI voice agent trained on Le Marquier's full product catalog, order management system, and troubleshooting documentation. The agent handles inbound calls 24/7, answering product questions, providing order status updates, and walking customers through common troubleshooting steps. When a call requires human expertise, it transfers seamlessly to the right team member with full context.

The results

  • 80% reduction in support costs. The AI voice agent now handles the bulk of incoming calls, dramatically reducing the hours required from human agents.
  • 98% call handling rate. Only 2% of calls require human escalation. The voice agent resolves nearly everything on its own.
  • Staff freed for high-value work. The support team now focuses on B2B relationships, complex technical issues, and proactive customer outreach. Revenue-generating activities, not repetitive Q&A.
  • 24/7 availability. Customers get instant answers at 11pm on a Saturday. No hold times. No voicemail.

Case Study #2: Plumbing Company Captures 40% More Leads

The business

A local plumbing company with 12 technicians serving a mid-size metro area. They run Google Ads and rely heavily on inbound phone calls for new business.

The challenge

The owner knew he was leaving money on the table. After-hours calls were going to voicemail, and most of those callers never called back. They called the next plumber on the list. During business hours, the office manager was juggling scheduling, dispatch, and phone calls simultaneously. Lead response time averaged 4 hours. For emergency plumbing, that is an eternity.

The solution

We built a two-part system. First, an AI phone agent that answers every call, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. The agent qualifies the lead (service needed, location, urgency), provides a rough estimate range, and books the appointment on the spot. Second, an N8N workflow that instantly pushes each new lead into the CRM, assigns it to the right technician based on location and availability, and sends the customer a confirmation text with the tech's name and arrival window.

The results

  • 40% more leads captured. Every call gets answered. Every after-hours caller becomes a booked appointment instead of a missed opportunity.
  • Response time dropped from 4 hours to 30 seconds. The AI agent picks up on the first ring. The CRM gets updated instantly. The technician gets notified immediately.
  • Zero missed calls. Weekends, holidays, 3am pipe bursts. Every call gets handled.
  • Office manager reclaimed 2+ hours/day. No more juggling phone calls while trying to manage dispatch. She now focuses on vendor relationships and billing.

Case Study #3: Real Estate Agency Saves 15 Hours/Week Per Agent

The business

A real estate agency with 8 agents, focused on residential sales and rentals. They close 15 to 20 transactions per month across the team.

The challenge

Each agent was spending 3+ hours per day on administrative work: sending follow-up emails to leads, processing intake documents, updating the CRM after showings, and coordinating schedules with buyers, sellers, and inspectors. That is 15+ hours per week per agent that could have been spent on showings, negotiations, and closings.

The solution

We built a suite of N8N automations covering the three biggest time sinks:

  • Document processing. When a new client submits intake forms, the workflow extracts key data, populates the CRM, creates the client folder, and generates the initial paperwork. What used to take 45 minutes now takes zero agent time.
  • Email sequences. Automated, personalized follow-up sequences triggered by lead stage. New inquiry? They get a welcome email with relevant listings within 2 minutes. Post-showing? A follow-up with comparable properties and next steps. No agent has to remember to send a thing.
  • Appointment scheduling. Buyers can book showings directly through a scheduling link that checks agent availability, property access windows, and drive time between appointments. Confirmations and reminders go out automatically.

The results

  • 15 hours/week saved per agent. That is nearly two full workdays reclaimed for revenue-generating activity.
  • 2x more property showings. With admin work automated, agents are doing double the showings they were before.
  • Faster lead response. New inquiries get a personalized response in under 2 minutes, compared to the previous average of 6 hours.
  • Zero dropped follow-ups. Every lead gets the right email at the right time. No one falls through the cracks because an agent got busy.

Common Patterns Across All Three Businesses

These are three different industries with three different challenges. But the patterns are identical:

  • ROI showed up within 30 to 60 days. None of these businesses waited 6 months to see results. The plumbing company saw increased lead capture in the first week. Le Marquier's support costs dropped within the first month.
  • The biggest wins came from automating the most repetitive work. Not the complex, judgment-heavy tasks. The stuff that burns out your best people. Answering the same questions. Sending the same emails. Entering the same data.
  • AI did not replace a single employee. In every case, staff were freed to do higher-value work. The plumbing company's office manager moved to vendor management. Le Marquier's support team pivoted to B2B sales. Real estate agents doubled their showing volume.
  • They started with one automation, proved it worked, then expanded. Le Marquier started with voice. The plumbing company started with lead capture. The real estate agency started with document processing. Once the first win was proven, expanding was easy.

Calculate Your Potential ROI

Every business is different, but the math is straightforward. Here is the framework we use:

Time savings: (Hours saved per week) x (Hourly cost of the person doing that work) x 52 weeks = Annual savings from time alone.

Revenue impact: (Additional leads captured per month) x (Average close rate) x (Average deal value) = Additional annual revenue.

Total ROI: Annual savings + Additional revenue - Cost of automation = Net annual return.

How long does it take to see ROI from AI automation?

More complex workflow automations typically break even within 60 days and continue generating compounding returns as the team adapts to the new processes.

Do I need technical skills to implement AI automation?

No. In each of these case studies, the business owners had no technical background. An AI automation agency handles the entire build, deployment, and optimization process. No. In every case study above, zero employees were let go. AI automation handles the repetitive, low-value tasks that burn out your staff.
